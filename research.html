Blood-brain barrier




Blood-brain barrier (BBB) acts as a highly selective physiological and chemical gate for protecting the brain from pathogens and toxins in a healthy human body. Under disease conditions, however, it becomes an obstacle that needs to be overcome for delivering the drugs since many small molecules (>400 Da), large biologics and proteins can not cross the BBB. More than 99% of the therapeutic agents falls into this population of drugs that cannot cross the BBB thus making the systemic delivery of drugs for CNS treatment extremely challenging. There is a great need of a model that mimicks the in vivo conditions and function of the BBB to be able to screen therapeutics that are enable to cross the BBB. Therefore, the objective of this project is to model a physiologically relevant in vitro BBB models utilizing Organ Chip technology to study transport mechanisms on the BBB and develop transcytosis assays for drug development. This project involves several sub-aims and branches to reach the ultimate goal of modeling healthy and disease models of the BBB, and develop shuttling agents for treatment of central nervous system (CNS) diseases.
  • Developing microfluidic in vitro human BBB Chip model with blood flow
  • Characterization of iPS-derived brain microvascular endothelial cells comparing their morphology, biomarker expression, and function to in vivo conditions
  • Developing antibody transcytosis assays on the BBB Chip for screening of the antibodies targeting a brain specific receptor
  • Studying peptides and small molecules that are opening the BBB reversibly for drug delivery purposes
  • Investigating the effects of the microbiome changes in the intestine to the BBB integrity
  • Developing BBB-Alzhimer Disease Chip model
  • Developing ischemic perfusion model on the BBB Chips

Site-specific functionalization of antibodies and Fab fragments for enhanced diagnostic applications

Objective: Immobilization of antibodies and their Fab fragments in an oriented manner to small scalled diagnostic surfaces such as microfluidic devices and nanoparticles while preserving their antigen binding activity and structure stability.

Antibody purification

The objective of this project is to design a novel affinity chromatography techniques for purification of monoclonal and polyclonal antibodies from cell culture media and ascites fluids. This method utilizes the nucleotide-binding site (NBS) that is located on variable domain of Fab region nearly all antibodies to facilitate capturing antibodies on a membrane affinity column.
Publications:

  • Nur Mustafaoglu, Tanyel Kiziltepe, Basar Bilgicer (2016). Antibody purification via affinity membrane chromatography method utilizing nucleotide binding site targeting with a small molecule. Analyst, 141, 6571-6582.
